Discover this masterpiece by Mother Nature during your Tanzania safari and leave with memories that will last forever.

About Tarangire

Serengeti National Park is Africa’s most famous park due to the largest concentration of game on the continent and the migration of millions of wildebeest. The most impressive nature documentaries were shot in this beautiful park. ‘Serengeti’ comes from the Maasai language meaning ‘endless plains’ and this rightly describes it. With almost 15,000 km2 (6,000 sq miles), Serengeti National Park is the second-largest park in Tanzania.

Place Of Elephant To Be Proud

Apart from elephants, Tarangire is a paradise for bird lovers. From parrots, hornbills, lovebirds and lilac breasted rollers to Kori bustards, eagles and vultures. The swamps in Tarangire National Park have one of the largest number of breeding bird species in the world. In the area around these swamps you can also see lions, leopards, cheetahs and even tree-climbing pythons.

Elephants are truly the architectural masterpieces of the African wilderness, standing as living monuments of power and grace. They move across the endless plains with a quiet dignity, their massive presence commanding respect from every other creature in the savanna. There is a profound sense of pride in the way a bull elephant holds his head high, tusks gleaming under the Serengeti sun, and in the way a matriarch leads her family with unwavering wisdom.
