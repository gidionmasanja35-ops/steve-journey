MIKUMI NATIONAL PARK

Mikumi National Park is one of the southern national park in Tanzania. Its open horizons and abundant wildlife draws frequent comparisons to the more famous Serengeti Plains. The park borders the Selous Game Reserve to the south and the two areas form a unique ecosystem. The chance that you will encounter lions climbing trees here is greater than in other parks!

Wildlife in the Mikumi National Park

Mikumi National Park is Tanzania's fourth-largest park, offering a stunning landscape often compared to the Serengeti. Located near Morogoro, its centerpiece is the Mkata Floodplain, where vast open horizons provide excellent wildlife viewing. This accessible destination serves as a vital gateway for travelers seeking an authentic and convenient African safari.

The park is home to diverse wildlife, including elephants, zebras, and the famous tree-climbing lions. Birdwatchers can enjoy over 400 species, while the popular hippo pools offer close-up views of these massive creatures. Surrounded by the Uluguru and Rubeho mountains, Mikumi provides a breathtaking backdrop for every nature lover’s adventure..

Birds in Mikumi

Mikumi is a good place for bird lovers as it is also home to more than 400 different species of birds! The Mkata River offers majestic views of fishing Open-billed Storks. Marabou stork, Red-billed oxpeckers, Black-bellied bustard, Francolin, Cattle egret, Guinea-fowls, Hornbill and Malachite Kingfischer are common birds in Mikumi National Park. The most colourful species are the Yellow-throated long claw, the Lilac-breasted roller and the Bateleur eagle. European migrants are common in Mikumi National Park during the rainy season.

Information and facts

Established in 1964, Mikumi National Park is the fourth largest in Tanzania, covering approximately 3,230 square kilometers. Often called the "Little Serengeti" due to its open savannahs and diverse wildlife, it is a key part of the larger Selous-Nyerere ecosystem, allowing animals to move freely between the reserves.

Fact About Mikumi

The park is home to four of the "Big Five"—lions, leopards, elephants, and buffalo—along with giraffes, zebras, and over 400 bird species. Its most famous feature is the Mkata Floodplain, where visitors frequently spot the world’s largest antelope, the eland, and unique tree-climbing lions resting in the acacia trees.
